“Apple”

Glub glub glub

You ever play mermaids in the pool? I didn’t. Until 7 years ago, after a fateful flight. The Mermaid (美人鱼). I have very little recollection of any of the events that occurred in the movie. There are flash scenes of moped rides by the cliffside, the mermaid fell in love with the big bad pollution man (who was played by 邓超 with a weird stache), and there might be a guy who’s like Ursula but less purple? I remember it being quite funny. 


Nevertheless, this single movie changed the trajectory of my life. Maybe it’s the fact that I was at a very impressionable age at the time, or perhaps the sleep deprivation made the movie seep into my subconscious, but either way, this is the movie that taught me about humanity’s impact on climate change. This is the movie that started my professional career in doomscrolling. This movie made my mother explain the complex topic of climate change to me at 3 a.m. EST. This movie gave me an eighth-life crisis 35,000 feet in the air. 


So, what’s the takeaway? Don’t toss your Rold Gold pretzel bag onto the rocks by the goats. Try not to scream too loudly at the fish. Compliment a squirrel every now and then. Say hello to the crows on top of the science center. Honk back at the geese. Appreciate lumpsuckers more.

(aforementioned lumpsucker)


Have a good day. Try not to think about Season 3 of Star Trek. Because what was up with that episode with the possessed children? I’d love to pick the brain of whoever wrote that, but I think I’d come out on the losing side of that conversation. I’ve rambled for too long. I leave you be. Spread your wings like a Hoatzin and soar.
